Empathy & Resilience

⛅ Stormy Day

Anaya loved school — especially playing outside during recess and showing her colorful drawings in art class. She always tried to be kind, helpful, and cheerful. Most days were full of laughter and high-fives. But one day, something strange happened.

At lunch, Amira brushed past her without saying hello. Later, during a game of tag, Amira snapped, "Just move already!" Anaya blinked and felt her tummy twist. Amira had never talked to her like that before. "What did I do wrong?" Anaya wondered.

That evening, her big brother Arjun noticed she was quiet. "You know, sometimes when people are upset or unkind, it doesn't mean you did something wrong. It might not even be about you at all. Maybe Amira was having a hard day."

"But she acted like I was the problem," Anaya said softly. Arjun smiled. "People often carry heavy feelings that we can't see. You might've just been nearby when her storm came."

Anaya thought about it that night. Maybe Amira's grumpiness wasn't really about the game or lunch. The next day, Anaya took deep breaths and didn't let Amira's words stick to her heart. She gave a friendly wave and went to play with Jamal, who always told funny jokes.

Later that week, Amira came up to Anaya and said, "Hey, I'm sorry I was weird to you. I was mad because my dog was sick, and I couldn't stop worrying." Anaya smiled kindly. "It's okay. I hope your dog feels better soon."

From that day on, Anaya remembered that sometimes people act a little stormy on the outside because of things happening inside. It wasn't always her storm to fix — or to carry.

🌟 Moral of the StoryNot everything is about you — and that's okay. When someone is unkind or distant, it often says more about their own struggle than about you. Don't carry their clouds. Keep walking in your light.
